# Skeptics & Counter-Arguments to BCI Disruption

## Key Skeptical Voices

| Expert | Affiliation | Key Argument |
|--------|------------|--------------|
| Miguel Nicolelis | Duke University | Brain is not computable; "downloading" is neuroscience fiction |
| Gyorgy Buzsaki | NYU | Neural codes are internal and idiosyncratic; no universal brain language |
| Karl Friston | UCL | Brain is a predictive model, not a storage device |
| Andrew Schwartz | U. Pittsburgh | Current BCIs decode only crude signals from tiny neuron samples |
| Hubert Dreyfus | UC Berkeley | Expertise is embodied and intuitive, not propositional |
| Bryan Caplan | George Mason | Education's value is signaling, not knowledge transfer |
| Philip Tetlock | U. Pennsylvania | Long-range societal predictions are unreliable |
| Nassim Taleb | NYU | Linear extrapolation fails in complex systems |
| Michael Polanyi | (historical) | Tacit knowledge cannot be articulated or digitized |

## "Knowledge Isn't the Bottleneck" Argument
- Knowing surgery theory doesn't make you a surgeon
- Wikipedia memorized = trivia champion, not competent professional
- Competence is NOT an information problem
- Daniel Kahneman's System 1 built through practice, not info transfer

## Biological Limitations
- 1,024 electrodes vs 86 billion neurons = 7 orders of magnitude gap
- Neural coding problem unsolved (firing rates? spike timing? oscillatory phase?)
- Foreign body response/gliosis degrades signals in 1-3 years
- Writing to brain orders of magnitude harder than reading
- Brain's plasticity may degrade any written patterns

## Historical Precedent of Overpromising
- AI Winters (1970s, 1980s): "human-level AI by 1980" predictions
- VR hype cycles (1990s, 2012, 2021): Meta lost $40B+
- Google Glass (2013-2015): killed by social rejection
- Amara's Law: overestimate short-term, underestimate long-term
- BCI appears near "peak of inflated expectations" on Gartner Hype Cycle

## Education System Resilience
- Schools serve: socialization, credentialing, critical thinking, childcare, social sorting
- Printing press, radio, TV, internet, MOOCs, Khan Academy all predicted to replace schools — none did
- Schools in 2025 structurally recognizable vs 1925

## Technology Adoption Realities
- 66% of Americans would NOT want a brain chip (Pew, 2016)
- Religious/cultural objections for billions
- 2.6B people still lack internet; 700M lack electricity
- Rogers' Diffusion: mass adoption takes decades
- Medical device regulation (FDA Class III) = years/decades for approval
- Collingridge Dilemma: when effects predictable, too late to control
