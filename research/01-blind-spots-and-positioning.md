## 7 Specific Blind Spots

### 1. The Institutional Transition, Told from the Ground Up
No sustained, granular narrative about how actual institutions — a specific school district, university, trade union, town — navigate the transition. Nobody shows the school board meeting where they debate neural links during exams.

### 2. The Complete K-12 Through University Question
Academic education literature has NOT seriously asked: what is school for if students can access any knowledge directly? Nobody covers: what happens to degrees, teachers, homework, grades, the developmental argument for/against children's BCI.

### 3. Physical/Hard-to-Automate Work in a BCI World
The cultural status inversion of trades vs knowledge work is unexplored. Nobody has written about the plumber who now earns more than the lawyer, the apprentice who downloads theory but still needs hands.

### 4. Optimistic Problem-Solving Tone (The Andy Weir Gap)
Existing BCI fiction: dystopian (Gibson, Anderson), philosophical (Chiang), thriller (Naam), prophetic (Kurzweil). NOBODY has written warm, character-driven, problem-solving BCI fiction. PHM tone has never been applied to this subject.

### 5. The "What Do We Do All Day?" Question
Existing works focus on power/control. Few explore: if you don't need school or study, what does a Tuesday look like? How do people find purpose?

### 6. The German/European Perspective
The Bildung tradition, dual system (Duales System), Meister/apprentice, Handwerkskammer — fundamentally different starting point for "what is education for?" Never explored in BCI fiction.

### 7. The "During" — Not Before, Not After
Most fiction is set just before transformation or well after. The messy middle decade — some students with BCIs, some without; universities panicking; tradespeople discovering new value — is almost entirely unoccupied.

## What Makes THIS Book Unique
The specific intersection of:
1. Universal BCI+AI (not elite-only)
2. Complete education pipeline (K-12 through university, including vocational)
3. The Great Inversion (physical work > knowledge work)
4. The meaning/purpose question for everyday people
5. Optimistic but not naive (PHM style)
6. Grounded in real neuroscience + AI research
7. Set DURING the transition, not before or after
8. Potentially European/German perspective

...does not exist anywhere in fiction or non-fiction.
